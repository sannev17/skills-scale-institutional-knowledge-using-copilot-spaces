# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers champion user needs throughout the product lifecycle. They conduct research, create wireframes and prototypes, run usability studies, and ensure that the delivered product is intuitive and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research (interviews, surveys, usability tests)
- Design low-fidelity and high-fidelity wireframes and prototypes
- Validate designs with users and stakeholders before development begins
- Coordinate design handoffs and maintain a shared design system
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design standards

### Goals
- Deliver experiences that meet real user needs and reduce friction
- Shorten feedback loops between design and development
- Ensure designs are feasible and consistent with product direction

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs with stakeholders
- Annotated mockups and design specifications for Developers
- Usability test reports shared with Product Managers and Project Managers

### Key Interactions
| Role | Nature of Interaction |
|---|---|
| Product Manager | Define user needs, align on personas/user stories, validate problem framing |
| Developers | Share design specs and assets; review implemented UI for fidelity |
| Project Manager | Align on design milestone dates and flag usability risks that affect timeline |

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts bring in-depth knowledge of a specific business domain, regulatory environment, or technical specialty. They ensure that requirements, designs, and deliverables are accurate, compliant, and feasible within their area of expertise.

### Responsibilities
- Review and validate requirements for domain accuracy and completeness
- Advise on feasibility, regulatory constraints, and business rules
- Support risk assessments by identifying domain-specific threats
- Participate in acceptance reviews and sign-off processes
- Provide training or documentation for domain-specific features

### Goals
- Prevent costly rework caused by misunderstood domain requirements
- Ensure delivered solutions comply with applicable standards and regulations
- Build shared understanding of the business domain across the team

### Typical Communication
- Ad-hoc consultations with Project Managers and Developers for clarification
- Written domain guidance, glossaries, or FAQ documents
- Participation in requirements workshops and design reviews

### Key Interactions
| Role | Nature of Interaction |
|---|---|
| Project Manager | Flag domain risks and constraints early; participate in risk review sessions |
| Product Manager | Validate problem framing and acceptance criteria against domain reality |
| Developers | Answer domain-specific questions and review implementation details |

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master (for Agile teams) serves the team by facilitating Agile ceremonies, removing impediments, and coaching team members on Agile best practices. They protect the team's ability to deliver value consistently and sustainably.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, sprint review, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that impede the team's progress
- Coach team members on Agile principles and continuous improvement
- Track and communicate team health, velocity, and capacity trends
- Shield the team from external interruptions during sprints

### Goals
- Maintain a predictable and sustainable delivery cadence
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Maximize team focus and minimize waste in the delivery process

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation and asynchronous blocker updates
- Sprint metrics and burndown charts shared with Project Managers
- Retrospective action items and coaching notes

### Key Interactions
| Role | Nature of Interaction |
|---|---|
| Project Manager | Align on delivery commitments, escalate unresolved blockers, share sprint metrics |
| Product Manager | Ensure backlog is refined and prioritized before sprint planning |
| Developers | Coach on Agile practices, facilitate daily standups, unblock day-to-day issues |

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and the delivery team. They elicit, document, and manage requirements, translating business needs into clear and actionable specifications that Developers and QA can work from.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit requirements through interviews, workshops, and document analysis
- Produce structured requirements artifacts (user stories, process maps, use cases)
- Define acceptance criteria and support QA in developing test cases
- Manage change requests and assess the impact of scope changes
- Facilitate communication between stakeholders and the delivery team

### Goals
- Ensure the team builds the right thing by clarifying requirements upfront
- Reduce ambiguity and rework through thorough specification
- Maintain a living requirements baseline that reflects approved changes

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User story documents, BRDs, and acceptance criteria shared with the team
- Change request logs reviewed with Project Managers and Product Managers

### Key Interactions
| Role | Nature of Interaction |
|---|---|
| Product Manager | Clarify business goals and product strategy; align on prioritization |
| Project Manager | Communicate scope changes and impact on timeline or budget |
| Developers | Provide detailed specifications and clarify requirements during development |
